Dear Patient,
I want to summarize your recent care in a clear and supportive way.
You have adenocarcinoma of the pancreas, which is a type of pancreatic cancer. Based on your history and prior scans, this cancer has spread (metastatic disease), meaning it has moved beyond the pancreas.
You first started treatment with FOLFIRINOX (a combination of chemotherapy drugs). After several cycles, a spot was seen in the liver that was concerning for spread. You then switched to another chemotherapy treatment called gemcitabine and Abraxane. You completed six cycles of this treatment.
At this time, you are on surveillance, which means we are closely monitoring the cancer with scans and visits, rather than giving active chemotherapy.
Your most recent CT scans show that the pancreatic mass is stable in size, and there is no clear evidence of new spread of cancer. This is an encouraging sign that the disease is under control.
Recently, you had a procedure called ERCP (a procedure to open blocked bile ducts) because of nausea. Doctors found blockage in your bile duct stent from tumor growth, stones, and clots. These were removed, and a new stent was placed to help bile flow normally. After this, you are feeling much better, with improved energy and appetite.
You have shared some symptoms, including mild “brain fog” and some numbness in your feet, likely related to prior chemotherapy.
Next steps:
* Continue close monitoring (surveillance)
* Repeat CT scan in about 2 months
* Follow up with your care team to review results and discuss options if needed
