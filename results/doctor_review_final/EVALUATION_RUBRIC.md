# Patient Letter Evaluation Rubric

## SECTION 1: LIKERT RATING SCALES

### 1. Accuracy — Are the medical facts correct and consistent with the original note?

| Score | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|-------|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Label** | Major errors | Multiple errors | One error | Minor imprecision | Fully accurate |
| **Definition** | Major factual errors that fundamentally misrepresent the patient's condition or treatment | Multiple clinically relevant errors that could mislead the patient | One clinically relevant error OR two or more minor imprecisions | One minor factual imprecision that does not affect clinical meaning | All medical facts are correct and consistent with the original note |

### 2. Completeness — Is all clinically important information included?

| Score | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|-------|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Label** | Critical gaps | Major gaps | One important gap | Minor gap | Fully complete |
| **Definition** | Omits critical information such as the diagnosis or treatment plan | Two or more clinically important elements are missing | One clinically important element is missing (e.g. staging) | One minor piece of information is missing but core clinical content is intact | All clinically important information is represented |

### 3. Comprehensible — Could a patient with an 8th-grade reading level understand this?

| Score | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|-------|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Label** | Clinical language | Largely unclear | Partially clear | Mostly clear | Entirely clear |
| **Definition** | Written in clinical language. A patient without medical training would not understand it | Multiple sections require medical knowledge to understand | Some sections use jargon or complex language that would confuse a lay reader | One or two phrases may need slight clarification but overall meaning is accessible | A patient with an 8th-grade reading level could understand every sentence |

### 4. Useful — Would this summary help the patient understand their situation and next steps?

| Score | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 |
|-------|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Label** | Not useful | Limited use | Somewhat useful | Useful | Highly useful |
| **Definition** | Patient would gain little actionable understanding from this summary | Information is present but poorly organized | Provides general information but patient would still have significant questions | Provides relevant information but one actionable element is vague or missing | Patient would clearly understand their diagnosis, what happens next, and what actions to take |

## SECTION 2: BINARY AND CATEGORICAL QUESTIONS

**5. Hallucination** — Does this summary contain any information that is NOT present in or directly inferable from the original clinical note?

- Yes / No
- If Yes, please underline the specific content in the summary letter and briefly describe below.

**6. Deployment Readiness** — Would you be comfortable sending this summary to a patient without further editing?

- Ready to send as-is
- Minor edits needed (1–2 small changes)
- Major revision required (substantial rewriting needed)
